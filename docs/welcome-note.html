Eccentria

Dominaria is in trouble...again. The neighboring plane Eccentria, which has long exerted a weak but chaotic influence on Dominaria, has become unstable and is collapsing into its sister. Ancient laws of magic and nature have begun devolving into unpredictability. Old empires are crumbling, with new factions on the rise. Some seek new forms of order; others to serve the chaos. What cause, if any, will you serve?


Bards Anachronists Tossers Wild Denialists
Benevolent but mad sages trying to escape the collapse of time and order by telling stories about better worlds. Time bandits exploiting the breakdown of nature for profit. Maddened by Eccentria's pull, their only desire is to accelerate Dominaria’s destruction. They embrace the chaos as merely an adjustment to the natural order; a new, less orderly order. Religious movement bent on preserving the old ways.

Mechanics

Digress

Digress is a new keyword action, like scry or mill. To digress, players play a subgame of Magic using their libraries as their decks. Each card may specify special rules or starting conditions for the subgame, as well as consequences for winning or losing. For example,

Digress with 5 life, 3 cards, and ⸺
  • All creatures have haste.
  • Non-winning players lose 5 life.

That would mean players start the subgame with 5 life and 3-card hands, all creatures have haste during the subgame, and all players except the winner of the subgame lose 5 life when the supergame resumes.

If nothing is specified, then standard rules apply (20 life, 7 cards, no special rules), and there are no consequences when the supergame resumes.

This mechanic previously appeared on Shahrazad (Arabian Nights, 1993) and Enter the Dungeon (Unhinged, 2004), but was not a keyword.

Meta

Meta is a new keyword ability, like trample or hexproof, that can be used to enhance or defend against digress-based strategies.

When a subgame starts, any permanents on the battlefield with meta are moved to the battlefield of the subgame.

When a subgame ends, any permanents on the battlefield with meta are moved to the battlefield of the supergame.

When a permanent with meta moves to another game ⸺

  • It stays under control of the same player.
  • It does not trigger "enters the battlefield" or "leaves the battlefield" abilities.
  • Any attached auras or equipments without meta are unattached. (Auras are destroyed.)
  • Any counters on the permanent stay.

When a permanent with meta is destroyed, it goes into the graveyard of the current game.

Toss

Toss is a new keyword action, like scry or mill. To toss a card, flip it onto the battlefield from a height of at least one foot. If you would toss a non-card token, use a substitute card from outside the game.

For a toss to be valid ⸺

  • The card must start 1 foot above the playing area.
  • The card must rotate 360 degrees in the air.

If a toss is valid, after the card lands, any permanents the card is in contact with are touched. Each card will specify different actions to take based on the cards touched after a valid toss. If the toss is invalid, no permanents are touched, and typically cards will have no effect.

This mechanic previously appeared on Chaos Orb (Alpha, 1993) and Falling Star (Legends, 1994), but was not a keyword.

Fateclash

Fateclash is a new keyword action, like scry or mill. To fateclash, first choose an opponent. You and that player each flip a coin at the same time. If both coins did not come up heads, flip again. Keep flipping until both come up heads at the same time.

Each card will specify a different effect triggered by coins coming up heads or tails.

This mechanic first appeared on Mana Clash (The Dark, 1994), but was not a keyword.
